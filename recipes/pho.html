Vietnamese beef Phó

Description

Vietnamese Phó, traditionally served with beef, is a national treasure. Actually it's the national dish of Vietnam. Don't quote me on that though.

The broth is very hearthy, fresh and full of flavours. Combine that with the fresh herbs, fresh cut meat and rice noodles and you have a tradional Phó. Eat it for breakfast, lunch or dinner. Phó does not discriminate.

Ingredients

Broth:

  • Beef broth
  • Yellow onion
  • Fresh ginger
  • Spices: ground coriander, cloves, fish sauce, hoisin sauce, soy sauce, red chili paste, cinnamon, salt and pepper.

Noodles

  • 8 oz. package of dried rice vermicelli noodles

Meat

  • ½ lb. sirloin steak, flank steak, brisket, or round eye, seasoned with salt and pepper

Garnishes

  • Green onions
  • Fresh jalapeño peppers or red Thai chili peppers
  • Fresh herbs (cilantro, basil leaves, mint leaves)
  • Fresh bean sprouts
  • Lime wedges
  • Sriracha hot sauce

Instructions

Make broth:

  1. Place a large dry pot over medium heat add the onion halves and ginger pieces and cook for 4 minutes.
  2. Add the broth, water, coriander, clove, fish sauce, hoisin sauce, soy sauce, chili garlic paste, cinnamon stick and a pinch of fresh cracked salt and pepper to the pot. Bring to a slow boil, then reduce heat and simmer for 30 minutes or more.

Prepare the noodles:

prepare rice noodles according to package instructions. Once soft, drain, rinse and set aside.

Prepare the garnishes:

Ready the garnishes in individual small bowls for serving: cilantro, basil, green onions, bean sprouts, lime wedges and sliced chilies.

Add meat to broth:

  1. 1-5 minutes before the broth is done cooking, add desired meat to the broth to cook, just until done (2-3 minutes for raw, thinly sliced chicken or pork, 1-2 minutes for shrimp or thinly sliced steak).
  2. Discard the ginger, clove, cinnamon stick and onion pieces from the pot.

Assemble:

Add cooked rice noodles to individual bowls and ladle hot broth over the top. Pile high with your favorite garnishes.
